Cultural critique

Whose body is the problem?

Clinical language often relocates the source of the difficulty into the individual body — "low desire," "vaginismus," "delayed ejaculation" — as though the symptom originated there. Purity-culture research suggests otherwise: the symptom is frequently the body's accurate memory of a system that made desire itself dangerous. Treating the presentation as a private deficit, without naming the ideology that produced it, quietly reproduces the very shame the client came in carrying.
